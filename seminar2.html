Graham Smith (2009): Democratic Innovations

  • Democratic theorists make strong arguments for increased citizen participation.
  • ...but pay less attention to how (at least on an insitutional level)
  • Political science on democratic institutions tend to focus on elections.
  • => Gap in the literature.

One exception to this

Percieved problem -> democratic theory -> (ideal) democratic institutions.

Some problems:

  1. Limited scope.
  2. Any democratic theory incomplete.
  3. ...democratic/participatory institutions already develop independently from democratic theories (my addition)

Smith's solution

Taking an ecumenical approach: An analytical framework that can encompass all participatory institutions, ie. "democratic institutions".

Analytical approach based on "democratic goods":

  • Inclusiveness
  • Popular control
  • Considered judgement
  • transparency
  • Efficiency
  • Transferability

But for this purpose, we need a defition of "democratic innovations".

Graham Smith's definition:

"Institutions that have been specifically designed to increase and deepen citizen participation in the political decision-making process."

  1. Directly engage citizens (so excluding organised interest groups)
  2. Institutionalised participation
    • Beyond the (hyper) local level.
    • Beyond "traditional" institutionalised engagement, ie. elections and consultations.
    • Excluding informal participation (protests etc.)

Question for you:

  • Do you agree that we need a analytical/theoretical framework about democratic innovations?
  • And why? What do we need it for?

Elstub & Escobar (2019): Typologising democratic innovations

10 years (and a lot of research) later, a new definition suggested:

  • "Processes or institutions...
  • ...that are new to a policy issue, policy role, or level of governance,
  • ...and developed to reimagine and deepen the role of citizens in governance processes
  • ...by increasing opportunities for participation, deliberation and influence."

Questions for discussion:

  • How does this definition differ from Graham Smith's?
  • Do you think this definition is an improvement? Why? Why not?
  • Is it too broad? Does it include too many things to still be meaningful? (concept stretching)

Typology of democratic innovations

Distinction between:

  • Ineliminable features
  • Quasi-Contingent features
  • Contextual features

Quasi-contingent features

These are heavily inspired by the thir text for today by Archung Fung.

  • Participant Selection Method
    • Purposive selection; Election; Sortition; Self-selection.
  • Mode of Participation
    • Observing; Listening; Voting; Discursive Expression.
  • Mode of Decision-making
    • No Decision; Aggregation of Preferences; Bargaining/Negotiation; Deliberation.
  • Extent of Power and Authority
    • Personal Benefits; Advise & Consult; Communicative Influence; Co-Governance; Direct Authority

Group exerrcise.

Discussion about quasi-contingent features

  • Do you find these features relevant? Did they miss something?
  • Are all the categories in each quasi-contingent feeature mutually exclusive?
  • Are the categories ordinal or categorical? Ie. can they be ordered from one extreme to another?
